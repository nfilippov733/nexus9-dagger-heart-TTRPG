# The Broker

*“Everything has a price. The trick isn’t knowing what it is; the trick is knowing who’s willing to pay it.”*

The Broker is a fixer, a smuggler, and a master of logistics. They know how to acquire rare goods, move them without attracting attention, and find the people who don't want to be found. In a galaxy where the flow of information and resources is tightly controlled by the major factions, the Broker operates in the grey areas, ensuring that the right people get the right things at exactly the right time.

Brokers are the lifeblood of the frontier and the underbelly of Nexus 9. They are former corporate executives who realized the real money was in the black market, independent traders who refuse to bow to the Syndicate, or information peddlers who trade in secrets rather than credits. They are defined by their connections, their resourcefulness, and their ability to turn a profit in any situation.

## Playing a Broker

**Your Role:** Utility / Controller
**Domains:** Sage, Arcana
**Base HP:** 6
**Base Evasion:** 10

**Hope Feature: Hidden Cache**
Spend 1 Hope to produce a common item that you "happened" to have on you, or to reveal a hidden compartment containing a small weapon or tool.

**Class Feature: Black Market Ties**
You can always find a buyer or a seller for illegal goods, regardless of your location. You gain Advantage on all Presence rolls to negotiate prices or secure rare items.

### The Broker in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Brokers are the people you go to when you need something done quietly. They are the ones who secure the forged transit papers, who find the untraceable weapons, and who know exactly which customs official is willing to look the other way.

**In Starship Combat:** Quartermaster. You are responsible for managing cargo, optimizing fuel consumption, and ensuring the ship's systems are running at peak efficiency.
**In Diplomacy:** Following the money. While the Envoy talks, you are identifying the black-market origins of the enemy's gear, bribing the guards, and finding the financial leverage needed to close the deal.

## Subclasses

### The Smuggler
The Smuggler focuses on hiding contraband, bypassing customs inspections, and moving goods across the galaxy without detection. They are the masters of the hidden compartment and the forged manifest.

**Foundation: Hidden Compartments**
You can conceal up to 3 small items on your person or in your ship that cannot be found by standard security scans or physical searches. You gain Advantage on all Finesse rolls to hide objects.

**Specialization: The Right Price**
You can spend 1 Hope to automatically succeed on a Persuasion roll to bribe a guard, official, or informant, provided you offer them something of value (credits, information, or a favor).

**Mastery: Untraceable**
Your ship and your personal comms cannot be tracked, intercepted, or recorded by any faction, regardless of their technological superiority. You are a ghost in the system.

### The Information Broker
The Information Broker focuses on acquiring information, forging documents, and using blackmail to achieve their goals. They trade in secrets, and they always know more than they let on.

**Foundation: A Guy Who Knows A Guy**
Once per session, you can declare that you have a contact in your current location who owes you a favor. This contact will provide valuable information or minor assistance without requiring a roll.

**Specialization: Blackmail**
When you interact with a named NPC, you can mark 2 Stress to reveal a piece of damaging information you hold over them, forcing them to comply with a reasonable request or suffer the consequences.

**Mastery: The Spider's Web**
You can spend 3 Hope to instantly know the location, current status, and immediate plans of any single individual in the galaxy, provided you have met them before or have a piece of their personal property.

# The Medic

*“I don’t care what uniform you’re wearing or who shot you. You’re on my table now, and that means you don’t get to die until I say so.”*

The Medic is a trauma surgeon, xenobiologist, and combat lifesaver, essential for keeping the crew alive in a hostile galaxy. In a universe where a single plasma burn or a ruptured environmental seal can mean agonizing death, the Medic is the line between survival and becoming another statistic. They are healers, scientists, and sometimes, the only people who truly understand the biological horrors lurking in the dark.

Medics are often former military doctors, researchers from the Aurelian Empire, or frontier healers who have learned to make do with whatever supplies they can scavenge. They are defined by their compassion, their clinical detachment, and their refusal to let anyone die on their watch.

## Playing a Medic

**Your Role:** Healer / Support
**Domains:** Splendor, Valor
**Base HP:** 7
**Base Evasion:** 9

**Hope Feature: Combat Triage**
Spend 1 Hope to heal an ally for 1d8 HP as a Free Action, provided you are within Melee range of them.

**Class Feature: Xenobiology**
You gain Advantage on all Knowledge rolls to identify alien toxins, diseases, or biological weaknesses, and on rolls to synthesize antidotes or treatments.

### The Medic in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Medics are the most respected and the most overworked people on the station. They are the trauma surgeons in the lower rings, the researchers studying the effects of Eclipse corruption, and the combat medics who patch up the Syndicate's enforcers no questions asked.

**In Starship Combat:** Chief Medical Officer. You are responsible for treating injuries during combat, managing life support systems, and ensuring the crew doesn't succumb to radiation or decompression.
**In Diplomacy:** Autopsies and analysis. While the Envoy talks, you are identifying the toxins used in an assassination, analyzing biological evidence, and determining the true cause of death.

## Subclasses

### The Combat Surgeon
The Combat Surgeon focuses on rapid healing, stabilizing critically injured allies, and keeping the crew fighting through impossible pain. They are the angels of the battlefield.

**Foundation: Triage**
When you heal an ally who is at 0 HP, they clear an additional 1d6 HP. You gain Advantage on all rolls to stabilize dying characters.

**Specialization: Stim-Pack**
When you heal an ally, you can mark 1 Stress to also grant them Advantage on their next action roll, as the combat stimulants flood their system.

**Mastery: Miracle Worker**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 5 Hope to completely restore an ally to full HP and clear all their Stress, even if they have just died (provided they died within the last minute and their body is mostly intact).

### The Xenobiologist
The Xenobiologist focuses on analyzing alien lifeforms, synthesizing antidotes, and turning the enemy's biology against them. They are scientists who use their knowledge as a weapon.

**Foundation: Anatomical Insight**
You gain Advantage on all Knowledge rolls related to alien biology, toxins, and diseases. When you succeed with Hope, you identify a critical weakness in the target's physiology.

**Specialization: Targeted Strike**
When you attack an organic enemy, you can mark 1 Stress to ignore their Armor Score by striking a vital nerve cluster, organ, or weak point you have identified.

**Mastery: Bio-Manipulation**
You can spend 3 Hope to alter the biology of a willing ally for the duration of the scene, granting them immunity to a specific damage type (e.g., acid, radiation, poison) or allowing them to breathe in a toxic atmosphere.
