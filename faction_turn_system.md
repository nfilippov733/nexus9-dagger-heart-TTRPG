# 4. The Faction Turn System

In *Nexus 9: The Fraying Dark*, the major powers do not sit idle while the players adventure. The galaxy is a living, breathing ecosystem of political maneuvering, espionage, and military escalation. To simulate this shifting landscape, the GM uses the **Faction Turn System**.

The Faction Turn is a mechanical procedure run by the GM between major campaign chapters or during significant periods of downtime. It determines what the major factions are doing off-screen, how their goals advance, and how their actions alter the state of the station and the galaxy.

## The Core Mechanics

Each major faction (Terran Coalition, Aurelian Empire, Kaelen Syndicate, Valari Collective, Mindclave, and Eclipse) has three key attributes:

1.  **Force:** The faction's military might, security presence, and capacity for direct violence.
2.  **Cunning:** The faction's espionage network, technological edge, and ability to operate in the shadows.
3.  **Wealth:** The faction's economic power, diplomatic influence, and logistical resources.

These attributes are rated from 1 to 5. When a faction takes an action, the GM rolls a number of d12s equal to the relevant attribute. The highest die determines the outcome.

### Faction Goals and Clocks
Every faction has a **Primary Goal** (a long-term objective, such as "Annex the disputed border colonies" or "Awaken the Eclipse sleeper agents") and a **Secondary Goal** (a short-term objective, such as "Assassinate the Terran Ambassador" or "Secure a monopoly on Progenitor artifacts").

These goals are tracked using **Clocks**—circles divided into 4, 6, or 8 segments. When a faction successfully completes an action related to a goal, they fill in segments on the clock. When the clock is full, the goal is achieved, and the state of the galaxy changes significantly.

## Running the Faction Turn

The Faction Turn consists of three phases:

### Phase 1: Faction Actions
In initiative order (determined by the GM based on the current narrative focus), each faction takes one Action. The GM chooses the action that best aligns with the faction's current goals and resources.

**The Action Menu:**
*   **Assault (Force):** Attack a rival faction's asset, territory, or personnel. *Success:* Inflict damage, seize territory, or fill 2 segments on a military goal clock.
*   **Espionage (Cunning):** Steal secrets, sabotage infrastructure, or assassinate a key figure. *Success:* Gain valuable intelligence, disrupt a rival's clock, or fill 2 segments on a covert goal clock.
*   **Diplomacy/Commerce (Wealth):** Negotiate treaties, secure trade routes, or bribe officials. *Success:* Form an alliance, gain a new asset, or fill 2 segments on an economic/political goal clock.
*   **Consolidate (Any):** Fortify existing positions, recruit new assets, or recover from losses. *Success:* Increase the relevant attribute by 1 (maximum 5) for the next Faction Turn, or clear negative conditions.

**Resolving the Action:**
The GM rolls a number of d12s equal to the faction's relevant attribute.
*   **1-5 (Failure):** The action fails. The faction suffers a setback, loses an asset, or a rival faction gains an advantage.
*   **6-9 (Mixed Success):** The action succeeds, but with a cost or complication. Fill 1 segment on the goal clock, but the faction is exposed, loses resources, or provokes a retaliation.
*   **10-12 (Full Success):** The action succeeds perfectly. Fill 2 segments on the goal clock.
*   **Critical (Two 12s):** The action is a spectacular success. Fill 3 segments on the goal clock and gain a permanent new asset or advantage.

### Phase 2: Player Interference
The players are not passive observers. During their adventures, they will inevitably interfere with faction goals. If the players directly thwarted a faction's plans during the previous chapter, the GM should remove 1 or 2 segments from that faction's relevant goal clock. Conversely, if the players unwittingly aided a faction, the GM should add segments.

### Phase 3: The News Feed
The results of the Faction Turn are not kept secret from the players. The GM translates the mechanical outcomes into narrative events that the players hear about through the station's news feeds, rumors in the Zocalo, or direct briefings from their contacts.

*Example: If the Kaelen Syndicate successfully uses Espionage against the Terran Coalition, the players might hear a news report about a massive data breach at Earthforce Command, or they might notice increased security patrols in the Blue Ring.*

## Escalation and Consequences

As faction clocks fill, the tension on Nexus 9 escalates. The GM should use the progress of these clocks to drive the campaign narrative. If the Aurelian Empire's "Annex Border Colonies" clock reaches 6/8, the players should feel the imminent threat of war. Station security will tighten, prices for weapons will skyrocket, and diplomatic channels will break down.

When a major clock is completed, it should trigger a significant event that directly impacts the players—a terrorist attack on the station, a blockade of the hyperspace gate, or the arrival of an Eclipse dreadnought. The Faction Turn ensures that the galaxy is always moving, always dangerous, and always reacting to the players' choices.
