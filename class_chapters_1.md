# The Envoy

*“A war is just a failure of imagination. Give me ten minutes in a room with their general, and I’ll give you a peace treaty. Give me twenty, and I’ll give you their fleet.”*

The Envoy is a master of diplomacy, espionage, and manipulation. They are the face of the crew, navigating the treacherous political waters of Nexus 9 and the wider galaxy. In a universe where a single misunderstood gesture can ignite a sector-wide conflict, the Envoy is the line between fragile peace and total annihilation. They do not win battles by outgunning the enemy; they win by ensuring the battle never happens, or by making sure the enemy is fighting themselves by the time the shooting starts.

Envoys come from all walks of life. Some are formally trained diplomats from the Aurelian Empire or the Terran Coalition, carrying the weight of billions of lives in their briefcases. Others are corporate negotiators, independent mediators, or even high-end grifters who have realized that governments pay better than marks. What unites them is an unparalleled understanding of people—what they want, what they fear, and what they will do to get it.

## Playing an Envoy

**Your Role:** Face / Social Specialist
**Domains:** Codex, Grace
**Base HP:** 5
**Base Evasion:** 10

**Hope Feature: Silver Tongue**
Spend 1 Hope to gain Advantage on any Presence roll during a negotiation, interrogation, or high-stakes social encounter.

**Class Feature: Web of Contacts**
Once per session, you can declare that you know an NPC in your current location who owes you a favor. This contact will provide information, minor resources, or temporary shelter, but will not risk their life or career without significant persuasion or payment.

### The Envoy in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Envoys are the most dangerous people in the room. A Marine can kill a squad; an Envoy can start a war that kills millions. They are constantly playing three-dimensional chess, balancing the demands of their home faction, the needs of their crew, and the overarching stability of the station.

**In Starship Combat:** Communications Officer. You are responsible for jamming enemy sensors, intercepting transmissions, and broadcasting demoralizing propaganda or false surrender codes.
**In Diplomacy:** The Primary Negotiator. You read motives, uncover political leverage, and dictate the terms of engagement.

## Subclasses

### The Ambassador
The Ambassador focuses on de-escalation, building alliances, and finding common ground. They are the stabilizing force in a chaotic galaxy, using their authority and empathy to prevent bloodshed.

**Foundation: Silver Tongue**
You gain Advantage on all Presence rolls made to negotiate, de-escalate conflict, or forge alliances. When you succeed with Hope on a negotiation roll, you also learn one of the target's hidden motives or fears.

**Specialization: Diplomatic Weight**
When you or an ally within Close range fails a social roll, you can mark 1 Stress to reroll the dice. You must accept the new result.

**Mastery: Galactic Treaty**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 4 Hope to force two warring factions or hostile groups in your presence to immediately cease hostilities and agree to a temporary truce. This truce lasts for the duration of the scene, provided neither you nor your allies take hostile action.

### The Provocateur
The Provocateur focuses on deception, disguise, and extracting secrets. They are agitators and spies, using misinformation and psychological warfare to dismantle their enemies from the inside.

**Foundation: Sow Discord**
You can spend 1 Hope to plant a rumor or lie that will be believed by the general public within a specific sector or station. The rumor must be plausible, though it does not need to be true.

**Specialization: Incite Rebellion**
You can mark 2 Stress to turn a crowd of civilians or low-level guards against their superiors for the duration of the scene. They will riot, protest, or create a massive distraction, allowing you and your allies to slip by unnoticed.

**Mastery: Character Assassination**
You can spend 3 Hope to completely destroy the reputation of a named NPC, stripping them of their rank, resources, and allies within their faction. This requires at least one scene of preparation (forging documents, leaking data, etc.).

# The Operative

*“You don’t need an army to take down an empire. You just need one person in the right place, at the right time, with the right knife.”*

The Operative is a specialist in stealth, sabotage, and targeted elimination. They operate in the shadows, dealing with problems before they become public. While the Envoy negotiates in the light, the Operative ensures the negotiations go smoothly by removing obstacles in the dark. They are ghosts, assassins, and infiltrators, trained to bypass the most advanced security systems in the galaxy.

Operatives are often former intelligence agents, black-ops soldiers, or corporate spies who have gone rogue or freelance. Some work for the Kaelen Syndicate as high-priced cleaners; others are deep-cover agents for the Terran Coalition. Regardless of their origin, they share a pragmatic, ruthless approach to problem-solving.

## Playing an Operative

**Your Role:** Striker / Infiltrator
**Domains:** Midnight, Grace
**Base HP:** 6
**Base Evasion:** 12

**Hope Feature: Vanish**
Spend 1 Hope to immediately enter stealth, even if you are being observed. You remain hidden until you attack, take damage, or interact with a complex object (like a computer terminal).

**Class Feature: Sneak Attack**
If you attack an enemy who is unaware of your presence, or who is currently engaged in melee with an ally, you deal an additional 1d8 damage.

### The Operative in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Operatives are the unseen hand moving the pieces on the board. They are the ones who steal the datapad before the summit, who plant the listening device in the ambassador's quarters, and who ensure the pirate captain never wakes up.

**In Starship Combat:** Electronic Warfare. You hack enemy systems, spoof sensors, and disable targeting locks.
**In Diplomacy:** Breaking and entering. While the Envoy talks at the front door, you are slipping through the ventilation shafts to acquire physical evidence or plant blackmail material.

## Subclasses

### The Ghost
The Ghost focuses on absolute stealth, bypassing security systems, and eliminating targets without leaving a trace. They are the ultimate assassins.

**Foundation: Active Camouflage**
When you are in dim light or darkness, you can spend 1 Hope to become completely invisible until you attack or take damage. While invisible, you gain Advantage on all Finesse rolls to move silently.

**Specialization: Silent Takedown**
When you attack an unaware target from Melee range, you deal an additional 2d6 damage. If this attack reduces a Minion or Standard adversary to 0 HP, they die silently without alerting others.

**Mastery: Shadow Walk**
You can teleport between areas of shadow or darkness up to Far range as a Free Action once per turn. You do not need line of sight to your destination, provided you know a shadow exists there.

### The Saboteur
The Saboteur focuses on explosives, traps, and disabling enemy technology. They are chaotic infiltrators who specialize in turning an enemy's fortress into a deathtrap.

**Foundation: Data Spike**
You gain Advantage on all Finesse rolls to hack terminals, bypass security doors, or disable alarms. When you succeed with Hope, you also download a piece of valuable encrypted data.

**Specialization: Rigged to Blow**
You can turn any small electronic device (a datapad, a commlink, a drone) into an explosive. It deals 3d6 energy damage to anyone within Very Close range when triggered remotely or by proximity.

**Mastery: Systemic Collapse**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 4 Hope to completely disable the security grid, lighting, and automated defenses of an entire facility or ship for the duration of the scene.
