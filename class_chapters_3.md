# The Engineer

*“A ship is just a million moving parts waiting to break. I’m the only reason they haven’t killed us yet.”*

The Engineer is a technical savant, capable of keeping a crippled ship flying, turning scavenged parts into deadly weapons, and bypassing the most advanced security systems in the galaxy. In a universe where survival depends on the integrity of a hull and the reliability of a life-support system, the Engineer is the crew's most vital asset. They are problem-solvers, inventors, and mechanics who see the world not as people and politics, but as systems and solutions.

Engineers are often former military technicians, corporate researchers, or self-taught prodigies from the frontier. They are defined by their ingenuity, their resourcefulness, and their ability to fix anything with a hydro-spanner and a roll of conductive tape.

## Playing an Engineer

**Your Role:** Protector / Support Tank
**Domains:** Valor, Blade
**Base HP:** 7
**Base Evasion:** 9

**Hope Feature: Jury-Rig**
Spend 1 Hope to temporarily repair a broken piece of equipment, bypass a locked door, or restore 1 Armor Slot to an ally.

**Class Feature: Shield-Bearer**
You can use your action to grant an ally in cover an additional +2 to their Evasion, or to project a localized energy shield that absorbs 1d6 damage for them.

### The Engineer in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Engineers are the unsung heroes who keep the station from spinning out of orbit. They are the maintenance crews in the Deep Core, the cyber-security experts for the major factions, and the black-market armorers who supply the Syndicate.

**In Starship Combat:** Chief Engineer. You are responsible for rerouting power, managing emergency repairs, and keeping the engines online when the ship takes a critical hit.
**In Diplomacy:** Analyzing technology. While the Envoy talks, you are recovering corrupted data, bypassing security feeds, and identifying the make and model of the enemy's weapons.

## Subclasses

### The Void Warden
The Void Warden focuses on personal armor, protecting allies, and absorbing damage. They are walking tanks, designed to hold the line and keep the crew safe.

**Foundation: Defensive Grid**
You can project a localized energy shield. Allies within Melee range of you gain +1 to their Armor Score, and you gain Advantage on rolls to resist being moved or knocked prone.

**Specialization: Reactive Shielding**
When an ally within Close range takes damage, you can mark 1 Stress to reduce that damage by your Armor Score. You can do this after the damage is rolled but before it is applied.

**Mastery: Aegis Protocol**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 4 Hope to make yourself and all allies within Close range immune to all damage for 1 round. The shield absorbs everything, from small-arms fire to ship-scale weaponry.

### The Combat Technician
The Combat Technician focuses on hacking, drones, and modifying weapons. They are battlefield controllers who use technology to outsmart and outgun the enemy.

**Foundation: Jury-Rig**
During a Short Rest, you can repair one broken piece of equipment or restore 1 Armor Slot to an ally for free, without needing tools or spare parts.

**Specialization: Drone Swarm**
You deploy a permanent companion drone. It can take the Help action once per round without consuming your action economy, granting an ally Advantage on their next roll.

**Mastery: System Overload**
You can spend 2 Hope to force any technological device, weapon, or vehicle within Far range to immediately shut down or malfunction for 1d4 rounds. This includes enemy cybernetics and powered armor.

# The Mystic

*“The universe is not empty space. It is a web of thoughts, emotions, and memories. I just know how to pluck the strings.”*

The Mystic is a telepath, a spiritual adept, or a technomancer capable of manipulating the minds of others or sensing the metaphysical currents of the universe. In a galaxy dominated by technology and firepower, the Mystic wields a power that cannot be bought, built, or easily defended against. They are the readers of minds, the seers of futures, and the manipulators of reality.

Mystics are often born into the Mindclave, trained from childhood to harness their psychic potential. Others are rogue telepaths hiding from the authorities, or Valari spiritualists who view their abilities as a divine gift. They are defined by their connection to the unseen world and the heavy toll that power takes on their minds.

## Playing a Mystic

**Your Role:** Ranged Damage / Telepathy
**Domains:** Arcana, Midnight
**Base HP:** 6
**Base Evasion:** 10

**Hope Feature: Mind Trick**
Spend 1 Hope to plant a brief, harmless suggestion in an NPC's mind, or to make them forget the last 30 seconds of conversation.

**Class Feature: Psychic Resonance**
You can communicate telepathically with any willing ally within line of sight, or with any ally you have previously bonded with, regardless of distance (provided they are on the same planet or station).

### The Mystic in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Mystics are both feared and highly sought after. They are the ultimate interrogators, the perfect spies, and the most dangerous wildcards in any negotiation. The Mindclave tightly controls registered telepaths, but rogue Mystics operate in the shadows, selling their services to the highest bidder.

**In Starship Combat:** Sensor Operator. You are responsible for sensor sweeps, detecting cloaked ships, and sensing the emotional state of enemy crews before they even open comms.
**In Diplomacy:** Reading surface thoughts. While the Envoy talks, you are detecting lies, sensing emotional imprints, and uncovering the hidden motives of the people across the table.

## Subclasses

### The Telepath
The Telepath focuses on precognition, sensing danger, and reading emotional residue. They are the ultimate investigators and manipulators of the mind.

**Foundation: Mind Reading**
You can spend 1 Hope to read the surface thoughts of a target within Close range for one minute. You gain Advantage on all social rolls against them during this time.

**Specialization: Psychic Assault**
When you deal energy damage with a Domain Card, you can mark 1 Stress to change the damage type to psychic and force the target to mark 1 Stress. This bypasses physical armor.

**Mastery: Mind Control**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 5 Hope to take complete control of an NPC's actions for the duration of the scene. They will follow your commands to the best of their ability, though self-destructive commands grant them a chance to break free.

### The Technomancer
The Technomancer focuses on telekinetic force and interfacing with machines. They bridge the gap between the biological and the artificial, controlling technology with their minds.

**Foundation: Machine Whisperer**
You can interface with computers, droids, and ship systems using your mind, requiring no physical connection or terminal. You gain Advantage on all Knowledge rolls to hack or slice.

**Specialization: Construct Override**
You can spend 2 Hope to take control of an enemy drone, turret, or synthetic for 1d4 rounds. It acts on your turn and follows your mental commands.

**Mastery: Ghost in the Machine**
You can permanently transfer your consciousness into a ship's mainframe or a secure network, allowing you to operate its systems as if they were your own body. You can return to your physical body as a Free Action.
