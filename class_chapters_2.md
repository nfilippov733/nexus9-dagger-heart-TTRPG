# The Pilot

*“The void doesn’t care who you are or what god you pray to. It only cares if you know how to fly.”*

The Pilot is an ace flyer, navigator, and survivalist, most comfortable behind the stick of a starship or a support vehicle. In a galaxy where the distance between habitable worlds is measured in light-years and the space between them is filled with pirates, anomalies, and the encroaching Eclipse, the Pilot is the only thing keeping the crew alive. They are thrill-seekers, adrenaline junkies, and consummate professionals who view a ship not as a machine, but as an extension of their own body.

Pilots come from the prestigious naval academies of the Terran Coalition, the brutal dogfighting arenas of the Kaelen Syndicate, or the solitary scout ships of the Valari Collective. They are defined by their reflexes, their spatial awareness, and their refusal to back down from impossible odds.

## Playing a Pilot

**Your Role:** Skirmisher / Vehicle Specialist
**Domains:** Bone, Sage
**Base HP:** 6
**Base Evasion:** 12

**Hope Feature: Evasive Action**
Spend 1 Hope to force an enemy to reroll a successful attack against you or your vehicle. You must accept the new result.

**Class Feature: Ace in the Hole**
You gain Advantage on all Agility rolls related to piloting, driving, or navigating a vehicle through hazardous terrain.

### The Pilot in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Pilots are the lifeblood of the station. They are the smugglers running blockades, the couriers delivering vital intelligence, and the fighter escorts protecting the massive trade convoys. Without them, the station starves.

**In Starship Combat:** Helm. You are responsible for evasive maneuvers, pursuit, and positioning the ship for optimal firing arcs.
**In Diplomacy:** Extraction planning. While the Envoy negotiates, you are analyzing flight logs, securing a fast exit route, and ensuring the ship is fueled and ready to burn.

## Subclasses

### The Ace
The Ace focuses on dogfighting, pushing vehicles beyond their limits, and outmaneuvering enemy fire. They are the undisputed masters of ship-to-ship combat.

**Foundation: Evasive Maneuvers**
When you are piloting a vehicle or ship, its Evasion increases by +2. If you are on foot, you gain +1 to your personal Evasion while moving.

**Specialization: Target Lock**
When you attack with a vehicle weapon, you can mark 1 Stress to gain Advantage on the roll. If the attack hits, you deal an additional 1d8 damage.

**Mastery: The Needle Threader**
You can navigate your ship or vehicle through impossible hazards (asteroid fields, closing blast doors, heavy crossfire) without needing to make a roll. You automatically succeed, though you may still need to spend Hope for particularly cinematic maneuvers.

### The Frontier Scout
The Frontier Scout focuses on exploration, surviving hostile environments, and tracking targets across the galaxy. They are the pathfinders who map the unknown.

**Foundation: Survivalist**
You gain Advantage on all Instinct rolls to track targets, find safe paths, or survive in hostile environments (extreme cold, radiation, toxic atmospheres).

**Specialization: Ambush Predator**
In the first round of combat, your movement speed is doubled, and your first attack deals an additional 1d8 damage if you act before your target.

**Mastery: Unseen Observer**
You can spend 2 Hope to perfectly blend into any natural or urban environment, becoming undetectable by sight, sound, or thermal sensors as long as you remain motionless. You can maintain this state indefinitely.

# The Marine

*“Diplomacy is what happens when the other side realizes they can’t win a firefight. I’m the reason they realize it.”*

The Marine is a shock trooper, trained for zero-G boarding actions, heavy firefights, and holding the line against overwhelming odds. They are the crew's primary physical deterrent, the armored fist that smashes through obstacles when negotiations fail. In a galaxy teetering on the brink of war, the Marine is the soldier who has already accepted that peace is an illusion.

Marines are the veterans of the Aurelian Legions, the heavy assault troopers of the Terran Coalition, or the hardened mercenaries of the frontier. They are defined by their discipline, their firepower, and their willingness to put themselves between their crew and certain death.

## Playing a Marine

**Your Role:** Frontline Combatant
**Domains:** Blade, Bone
**Base HP:** 6
**Base Evasion:** 11

**Hope Feature: Adrenaline Surge**
Spend 1 Hope to immediately clear 2 Stress or heal 1d4 HP as a Free Action.

**Class Feature: Combat Veteran**
You ignore the "Pinned Down" condition from suppressive fire, and you gain Advantage on rolls to resist being Frightened or Intimidated.

### The Marine in the Galaxy

On Nexus 9, Marines are the muscle that enforces the fragile peace. They are the station security officers, the private military contractors, and the bodyguards for high-ranking diplomats. When the Eclipse or the Syndicate makes a move, the Marine is the one who holds the line.

**In Starship Combat:** Tactical Officer. You are responsible for firing ship weapons, establishing target locks, and coordinating boarding actions or repelling boarders.
**In Diplomacy:** Physical intimidation. While the Envoy talks, you are analyzing the room for threats, identifying the enemy's heavy hitters, and making sure everyone knows what happens if the meeting goes south.

## Subclasses

### The Shock Trooper
The Shock Trooper focuses on close-quarters combat, breaching tactics, and overwhelming physical force. They are the tip of the spear in any assault.

**Foundation: Breaching Charge**
You gain Advantage on Strength rolls to break down doors, smash through barricades, or grapple enemies. When you successfully breach an obstacle, you and your allies gain +1 to your next attack roll.

**Specialization: Unstoppable Momentum**
When you reduce an enemy to 0 HP with a melee attack, you can immediately move up to Close range without triggering reactions, and make a second attack against a different target.

**Mastery: Juggernaut**
Once per Long Rest, you can spend 3 Hope to ignore all damage from a single attack, regardless of its severity. You simply shrug off the blow and continue fighting.

### The Heavy Gunner
The Heavy Gunner focuses on suppressive fire, area-of-effect weapons, and controlling the battlefield through sheer volume of fire.

**Foundation: Suppressive Fire**
When you attack with a two-handed ranged weapon, you can mark 1 Stress to force the target and all enemies within Very Close range of them to roll their next attack with Disadvantage.

**Specialization: Overcharge**
When you roll maximum damage on any damage die with a ranged weapon, you can reroll that die and add the new result to the total. You can continue doing this as long as you roll the maximum value.

**Mastery: Walking Artillery**
You can wield two-handed heavy weapons with one hand, allowing you to carry a shield or a secondary weapon without penalty. You also ignore the "Heavy" burden trait on all weapons.
