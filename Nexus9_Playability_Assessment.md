# Nexus 9: The Fraying Dark — Comprehensive Playability Assessment

**Date:** April 12, 2026
**Scope:** Manuscript content, website tools, Daggerheart system compliance, art coverage
**Verdict:** The project has a strong mechanical skeleton and excellent website tooling, but the manuscript is approximately 22% written. A group could run a one-shot with the pregen crew today, but a full campaign is not yet playable.

## 2. Can You Play Right Now?

### Yes — A One-Shot

A GM can run a single session today using:

1. The **Quickstart Rules** (1,941 words) — covers action rolls, combat, Hope/Fear, damage thresholds
2. The **Pregen Crew** (3,358 words) — 6 fully statted characters with equipment, domain cards, bonds, and secrets
3. The **Encounter Builder** on the website — 35 adversaries with difficulty, HP, and fear cost
4. The **Conditions Quick Reference** (769 words) — all combat and social conditions
5. The **Dice Roller** on the website — Hope/Fear 2d12 with critical detection

The GM would need to improvise adversary abilities (since stat blocks lack attack modifiers, damage dice, and features) and create their own scenario, but the mechanical framework is complete enough for play.

### No — A Full Campaign

A full campaign requires content that does not yet exist:

1. **No adversary abilities** — stat blocks are name/tier/difficulty/HP only. No attack actions, damage dice, special features, Fear Moves, or tactical behavior.
2. **No campaign chapters** — Book Seven is 232 words across 5 stub entries. No encounter sequences, scene breakdowns, NPC dialogue, or choice trees.
3. **No setting lore** — History, factions, culture, and daily life are all empty. Players and GMs have no world to inhabit.
4. **No class prose** — Class chapters are 20-25 word stubs. Players cannot read about what their class does or how it feels to play.
5. **No rest/recovery rules** — How do characters heal between encounters?
6. **No stress/breaking rules** — The stress system is referenced but never defined.

## 3. Daggerheart System Compliance

### What Aligns Correctly

| Daggerheart Element | Nexus 9 Implementation | Status |
|:---|:---|:---:|
| 2d12 Hope/Fear action rolls | Fully implemented in rules and website dice roller | Correct |
| Trait modifiers (6 traits) | Agility, Strength, Finesse, Instinct, Presence, Knowledge | Correct |
| Standard Array | +2, +1, +1, 0, 0, -1 | Correct |
| Damage Thresholds (Minor/Major/Severe) | 0/1/2/3 HP loss model, clearly explained | Correct |
| Armor Score system | Armor reduces damage before threshold comparison | Correct |
| Domain card system | 9 domains, 189 cards, levels 1-10 | Correct |
| Hope/Fear economy | Hope spent for abilities, Fear fuels GM moves | Correct |
| Range bands | Melee, Very Close, Close, Far, Very Far | Correct |
| Fear budget for encounters | Implemented in Encounter Builder | Correct |
| 10-level progression | Character builder supports levels 1-10 with tier scaling | Correct |
| 4-tier structure | Tier 1 (1-2), Tier 2 (3-4), Tier 3 (5-7), Tier 4 (8-10) | Correct |

### What Deviates or Is Missing

| Daggerheart Element | Issue | Severity |
|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Adversary stat block format** | SRD expects: Attack Modifier, Damage Dice, Difficulty, Damage Thresholds, HP, Features, Fear Moves. Nexus 9 has only: name, tier, difficulty, HP, fear cost, signature line. | **Critical** |
| **Class progression tables** | SRD has 10-level tables with 2-3 domain card choices per level. Nexus 9 has domain cards but no published progression tables showing which cards are available at which level per class. | **High** |
| **Subclass features** | SRD subclasses grant unique features at specific levels. Nexus 9 subclass data exists in the character builder but is not written up in the manuscript. | **High** |
| **Experiences system** | 14 experiences exist in the data layer. The manuscript does not explain how experiences work mechanically (bonus to specific rolls? narrative permission?). | **Medium** |
| **Stress and Breaking** | Referenced throughout but the chapter "Stress, Conditions, and Breaking" is empty (0 words). | **Critical** |
| **Rest and Recovery** | Empty chapter (0 words). No rules for short rest, long rest, or downtime healing. | **Critical** |
| **Death and Dying** | 181 words exist — a start, but needs expansion for edge cases. | **Medium** |
| **Weapon damage scaling** | SRD scales weapon damage by tier (+3/+6/+9). Nexus 9 weapons exist at each tier but the manuscript weapon tables are stubs (23-32 words each). | **Medium** |

### Adversary Stat Block Gap (Critical)

This is the single biggest playability blocker. The Daggerheart SRD expects adversary stat blocks to include:

| Field | SRD Requirement | Nexus 9 Status |
|:---|:---|:---:|
| Name | Required | Present |
| Type (Minion/Standard/etc.) | Required | Present |
| Tier | Required | Present |
| Difficulty | Required | Present |
| HP | Required | Present |
| Attack Modifier | Required | **Missing** |
| Damage Dice | Required | **Missing** |
| Damage Thresholds (Major/Severe) | Required | **Missing** |
| Features / Abilities | Required | **Missing** |
| Fear Moves | Required for Bosses | **Missing** |
| Stress infliction | Common | **Missing** |

Without attack modifiers, damage dice, and abilities, a GM cannot run combat. They would need to improvise every adversary's actions, which defeats the purpose of having stat blocks.

## 8. What the Website Does Exceptionally Well

Despite the manuscript gaps, the website is a genuinely strong digital companion:

1. **Character Builder** — Full 7-step pipeline with ancestry, community, class, traits, domains, gear, and review. Saved characters gallery. Level 1-10 with tier-aware gear filtering. This is better than many published TTRPG companion apps.

2. **189 Domain Cards** — Every card has a name, level, type, cost, and full mechanical effect. This is a complete card library that could be printed and played today.

3. **Encounter Builder** — 35 adversaries with fear budget calculator and difficulty scaling. The UI is clean and functional. It just needs the stat blocks fleshed out.

4. **Book Reader** — Full-text search, bookmarks, dice roller, quick reference, keyboard shortcuts, reading time, progress tracking. This is a market-standard digital reader.

5. **Pregen Crew** — 6 fully statted characters with equipment, domain cards, bonds, and secrets. This is the most complete content in the entire project and could anchor a quickstart release.
