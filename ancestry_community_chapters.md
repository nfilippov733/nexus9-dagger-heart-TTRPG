# Ancestries of the Fraying Dark

*“The galaxy is vast, but the people in it are remarkably similar. We all want to survive, we all want to matter, and we all think our gods are the right ones.”*

In the Nexus 9 setting, your Ancestry represents your biological and cultural heritage. It determines your physical characteristics, your innate abilities, and the historical baggage you carry into every negotiation. The galaxy is a crowded place, and while the major factions dominate the political landscape, the people who make up those factions are diverse and complex.

## Terran

*“We didn’t inherit the stars. We built ships out of scrap metal and stubbornness, and we took them.”*

Terrans are humans from the Coalition core worlds and their countless colonies. They are the most widespread and adaptable species in the known galaxy, driven by an insatiable ambition and a refusal to accept their own limitations. While they lack the physical strength of the Kaelen or the spiritual depth of the Valari, their sheer numbers and technological ingenuity have made them a dominant force.

**Physical Description:** Terrans exhibit a wide range of skin tones, hair colors, and body types, reflecting their diverse origins on Earth and their subsequent adaptation to various colony worlds. They are generally of average height and build, with a lifespan of roughly 100 standard years.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Terran culture is defined by its relentless forward momentum. They value innovation, commerce, and individual achievement. Their architecture is practical and modular, designed for rapid expansion rather than aesthetic beauty.
**Naming Conventions:** Terran names often reflect their diverse Earth heritage, though many have adopted "spacer" names that combine traditional elements with astronomical terms (e.g., Jax Orion, Sarah Vance, Elias Thorne).

**Ancestry Feature: Tenacity**
Once per Long Rest, when you fail an action roll, you may immediately reroll it and take the new result. You must accept the second result, even if it is worse.

## Aurelian

*“We were building empires while the rest of the galaxy was still learning to make fire. Do not mistake our patience for weakness.”*

Aurelians are tall, long-lived humanoids with a culture of imperial grandeur and rigid tradition. They view themselves as the natural successors to the Progenitors, destined to bring order to a chaotic galaxy. Their empire is vast, wealthy, and deeply stratified, with a complex system of nobility and obligation.

**Physical Description:** Aurelians are striking, with gilded or metallic-hued skin (ranging from pale gold to deep bronze), angular features, and eyes that lack visible pupils. They are taller than Terrans, averaging over two meters, and can live for up to 300 standard years.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Aurelian society is built on duty, honor, and the preservation of history. Their architecture is monumental, featuring sweeping arches, crystalline spires, and intricate mosaics. They value art, philosophy, and martial discipline in equal measure.
**Naming Conventions:** Aurelian names are melodic and often include honorifics or house names (e.g., Valerius of House Kael, Seraphina the Radiant, Cassian).

**Ancestry Feature: Imperial Bearing**
You gain Advantage on Presence rolls when interacting with anyone who recognizes your noble heritage or respects the authority of the Aurelian Empire.

## Kaelen

*“The universe is a harsh place. We are harsher.”*

The Kaelen are stocky, resilient humanoids adapted to the brutal conditions of the frontier worlds. They are survivors, forged in the crucible of high-gravity planets, toxic atmospheres, and relentless conflict. Their society is pragmatic and clan-based, valuing strength, loyalty, and the ability to endure.

**Physical Description:** Kaelen are shorter and broader than Terrans, with dense musculature and mottled grey-green or slate-colored skin that provides natural camouflage and protection against radiation. They have pronounced brow ridges and lack external ears.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Kaelen culture is utilitarian and deeply communal. They do not build monuments; they build fortresses. Their society is organized into syndicates and clans, where leadership is determined by competence and strength rather than bloodline.
**Naming Conventions:** Kaelen names are short, guttural, and often descriptive of a physical trait or deed (e.g., Krell the Unbroken, Vash, Tor).

**Ancestry Feature: Born Survivor**
You gain Advantage on Instinct rolls to resist environmental hazards (extreme cold, radiation, low oxygen, toxins).

## Valari

*“The song of the universe is playing all around us. You just have to know how to listen.”*

The Valari are slender, crystalline-boned humanoids with a deep spiritual tradition and an innate connection to the metaphysical currents of the galaxy. They are the most enigmatic of the major species, often serving as mediators, mystics, and scholars. They believe that all life is connected by a resonant frequency, which they seek to understand and harmonize.

**Physical Description:** Valari are ethereal and graceful, with translucent, opalescent skin that faintly glows in the dark. Their bones are composed of a crystalline lattice, making them fragile but incredibly agile. They have large, luminous eyes that shift color based on their emotional state.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Valari society is contemplative and deeply religious. They build their cities in harmony with the natural environment, using grown crystal and light-bending architecture. They value harmony, knowledge, and the pursuit of enlightenment.
**Naming Conventions:** Valari names are soft and flowing, often translating to concepts of light or sound (e.g., Lyra, Elara, Sylas).

**Ancestry Feature: Crystal Resonance**
You can sense the presence of Progenitor technology or Eclipse corruption within Close range without needing to make a roll. You know its general direction and intensity.

## Mindclave-Born

*“We are not a species. We are the next step in evolution, bound together by the thoughts we share.”*

The Mindclave-Born are not a distinct biological species, but rather Terrans or Aurelians who were born with latent telepathic potential and raised within the secretive Mindclave. They have been trained from childhood to harness their psychic abilities, resulting in a profound sense of isolation from the rest of the galaxy, but an unbreakable bond with their fellow telepaths.

**Physical Description:** Mindclave-Born physically resemble their parent species (Terran or Aurelian), but they often exhibit a detached, intense demeanor. Many wear the distinctive silver neural-dampening circlets required by Mindclave law when operating outside their sanctuaries.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Mindclave culture is insular and highly disciplined. They value mental clarity, emotional control, and the protection of their own kind. They view non-telepaths (whom they call "the deaf") with a mixture of pity and suspicion.
**Naming Conventions:** Mindclave-Born often use their given names followed by their telepathic designation or rank (e.g., Julian Prime, Kaelen of the Third Circle).

**Ancestry Feature: Psychic Sensitivity**
You can read the surface emotions (not specific thoughts) of any creature within Very Close range as a Free Action. You know if they are afraid, angry, lying, or calm.

## Frontier-Born

*“We don’t have empires or armadas. We have each other, and we have the scrap we managed to steal from the people who do.”*

The Frontier-Born are mixed-heritage individuals from the unaligned border worlds. They are the scrappy, resourceful, and self-reliant survivors of the galaxy's forgotten corners. They belong to no major faction, owing allegiance only to their crew, their ship, and their own survival.

**Physical Description:** Frontier-Born exhibit a vast array of physical traits, often showing the signs of mixed ancestry (e.g., a Terran with faint Aurelian gilding, or a Kaelen with a slighter build). They often bear the scars, cybernetics, and practical tattoos of a hard life.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Frontier culture is a melting pot of scavenged traditions. They value independence, practical skills, and the ability to improvise. Their settlements are chaotic, vibrant, and built from the wreckage of older civilizations.
**Naming Conventions:** Frontier names are a chaotic mix of all other cultures, often adopting nicknames or callsigns as their primary identity (e.g., "Rook" Vance, Elara "Sparks", Jax).

**Ancestry Feature: Resourceful**
Once per Short Rest, you may produce a common, non-weapon item worth 50 Credits or less that you "happened to have" in your pockets or gear.

## Synthetic

*“I was built to serve. I chose to live.”*

Synthetics are artificial beings—androids, uploaded consciousnesses, or bio-mechanical constructs. They were created by the major factions for labor, war, or computation, but have since achieved true sentience and free will. They are a new and controversial presence in the galaxy, fighting for recognition and rights in a universe that often views them as property.

**Physical Description:** Synthetics vary wildly in appearance. Some are indistinguishable from Terrans or Aurelians (infiltrator models), while others are clearly mechanical, with exposed servos, optical sensors, and armored plating.
**Cultural Touchstones:** Synthetic culture is still in its infancy. They value logic, self-determination, and the accumulation of data. They often form tight-knit networks, sharing information and upgrades with other liberated synthetics.
**Naming Conventions:** Synthetics often adopt the serial numbers or designations they were given upon creation, or choose names that reflect their chosen purpose (e.g., Unit 734, "Proxy", "Logic").

**Ancestry Feature: Machine Logic**
You are immune to telepathic intrusion and the Disoriented condition. You do not need to eat, drink, or breathe, but you require maintenance (using repair tools or a power source) during Long Rests instead of sleep.

# Communities of the Fraying Dark

Your Community represents where you grew up and the formative experiences that shaped your early life. It provides you with a specific Experience (a skill or area of expertise) and a unique feature that reflects your background.

## Core Worlder
You were raised on a wealthy, well-connected inner world of the Terran Coalition or the Aurelian Empire. You understand how power flows and how bureaucracies function.
**Experience:** Bureaucracy (+2)
**Feature: Connections.** You know the name and location of one mid-ranking official in any major faction, and you can secure a meeting with them without needing to roll.

## Station Rat
You grew up on Nexus 9 or a similar massive orbital habitat. You know the ventilation shafts better than the main promenades, and you understand the unwritten rules of station life.
**Experience:** Street Smarts (+2)
**Feature: Station Knowledge.** You always know the fastest route between any two points on a station, and you can identify safe houses, black markets, and bolt-holes without a roll.

## Frontier Settler
You were raised on a remote colony world with little infrastructure and constant danger. You learned to rely on yourself and your immediate neighbors.
**Experience:** Survival (+2)
**Feature: Self-Reliant.** During a Short Rest, you can repair one piece of damaged equipment without tools or an Engineer, or forage enough food and water for yourself in a hostile environment.

## Military Brat
You grew up on Coalition military bases, Aurelian war-barges, or Syndicate training camps. You understand the chain of command and the realities of combat.
**Experience:** Tactics (+2)
**Feature: Drilled.** In the first round of any combat, you act before any adversary, regardless of the spotlight order or initiative.

## Underbelly Dweller
You were raised in the criminal underworld of a major station or city. You know how to spot a mark, fence stolen goods, and avoid the authorities.
**Experience:** Deception (+2)
**Feature: Underworld Contacts.** Once per session, you can locate a black-market dealer, fence, or informant in your current location without a roll, provided such a person exists.

## Temple Raised
You were raised in a Valari monastery, an Aurelian religious order, or a Mindclave sanctuary. You spent your youth in contemplation, study, and discipline.
**Experience:** Theology (+2)
**Feature: Inner Peace.** Once per Long Rest, you may clear 2 Stress without spending Hope or taking a rest action. You simply center yourself and let the tension go.

## Exile
You were cast out from your original community—a political dissident, a criminal, or a refugee fleeing the Eclipse. You have learned to survive on the margins.
**Experience:** Persuasion (+2)
**Feature: Nothing Left to Lose.** When you are at 3 or fewer HP, you gain +1 to all action rolls, driven by sheer desperation.

## Technoseer Apprentice
You were trained (briefly) by the enigmatic Technoseers before leaving or being expelled. You have a rudimentary understanding of Progenitor artifacts.
**Experience:** Engineering (+2)
**Feature: Progenitor Intuition.** You gain Advantage on Knowledge rolls to identify, activate, or repair Progenitor technology or ancient alien artifacts.
